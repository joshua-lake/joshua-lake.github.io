What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

Margin

The margin is used to define the amount the space outside of an element. When we adjust these values, this will determine how far away our element will be from it's neighbours or edges of the web page. The more margin we have, the more distance between the element we are adjusting and other elements will be, and visa versa.

Using margin is great way to control the position of our elements on our page. By adjusting the margins, we can move our elements around our page. We can also create overlaps with different elements by giving our margins negative values.

Padding

Padding is the space inside the element. Increasing or decreasing this will adjust the size of your element, but maintain the size of the content within the element. Think of this as changing the size of the container around your content.

Padding is useful when we would like to create some empty space inside our elements, or maybe we've got a button we'd like to make a bit bigger and easier to click on.

Border

The border is the space between the margin and the padding. Think of it as a picture frame for your element!
