Understanding Emotional Intelligence


What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is the ability to identify, evaluate, control and express our own emotions, as well as recognising the emotions of other around us.


How is it different to IQ?

IQ refers to our intellectual intelligence, or how 'book smart' we are. It is determined through a series of tests that incorporate mathematical skills, memory, spatial perception, and language abilities. EQ is how well we are aware of our and others emotions, and how well we can communicate them in healthy ways.


Why is it important?

EQ is important because it benefits our ability to interact with others in a healthy way. If we can identify the way we and others are feeling, we can better guide our thinking and behaviour. This will result in healthier and happier relationships, in and out of the workplace.
