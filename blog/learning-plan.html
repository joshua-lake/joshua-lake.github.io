My learning Plan


What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

To establish a rewarding career, both personally and financially. Something that challenges and engages me, and provokes me to learn continously.


Strengths and limitations

  • Strengths
  • Attitude
  • I believe I've got a good attitude. I like getting stuck in and getting the job done. I feel I have a good problem solving mentality.
  • Work well with others in a team
  • I enjoy working in a team. To me, helping others is rewarding, and being able to ask others for help is valuable. There's a lot you can learn from your peers!
  • Personable
  • I like to think I'm polite, friendly, approachable and helpful.

  • Current limitations
  • Problem solving muscle
  • While I think I have the right attitude, the muscle may need some strenghthening!
  • Becoming overwhelmed
  • I've noticed sometimes when I come under pressure of workloads and deadlines I can freeze up and stress out. This blocks my flow, and can be detrimental to my learning and work output.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

  • Two non technical skills I'd like to improve would be:
  • Planning and preparing
  • Sometime I can get a bit overexcited and dive straight into a project or task. Being able to improve on my ability to evaluate and pre prepare I think would be valuable to me.
  • Decision Making
  • This is something I've been continously working on. When I look at senior colleagues I've worked with in the past, they tend to have a great ability to make decisions under pressure. This is something I admire and believe it contributes towards good leadership skills. I realise this comes with experience, but also think mindset and self confidence play a big role.

My commitment to how I will manage my workload in this programme

  • An explanation of how I will manage myself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives
  • I'm in a fortunate position where I can dedicate all my time toward EDA. Having a routine is hugely beneficial to me, getting up early and going to the gym wakes me up and sets me up for the day. I like working a 9-5 structure with a bit of flexability either end. It also means I can not feel guilty while enjoying my downtime as my learning is planned. While this is my routine, it is flexible. Should I need to work hours I normally wouldn't for the sake of a group project, that isn't a problem for me.

My commitment as to how and where I will seek help in a timely way

Ask Google ! If I'm still unsure, when approproiate, ask my fellow students for advice on a challenge, or speak with a facilitator if I'm really stuck or feel it may be a time consuming answer/solve.


A description of what I expect from the facilitation team

I expect the facilitation team to be supportive, encouraging and knowledgeable. A team of people that will help guide us on this intensive learning experience, and rather than pick us up, help us up, when we're flailing on the ground in a tail-spin.
